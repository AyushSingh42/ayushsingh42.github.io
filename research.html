Prior Research

Laboratory for Developmental Language Studies · Rutgers University · June – August 2024

PI: Dr. Kristen Syrett

As part of the Aresty Summer Science Program at Rutgers University, I investigated how children classify novel words that relate to the mental and emotional states of animate or inanimate objects. This work aimed to better understand how children learn new words, especially those related to mental states.

I developed stimuli and utilized the Children Helping Science program to create asynchronous studies, which increased the volume of data available for analysis. Through this research, I identified that children can recognize words correlating to the mental state of objects, even when the chosen words are arbitrary.

I presented this work as a poster titled "Learning Emotion and Mental State Adjectives from Linguistic Context" at the Aresty Summer Science Program in August 2024.
